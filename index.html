Hi. We are Group-57.

And this is our project, Analyzing the proliferation of fake news: "The Marcos family cleared ALL allegations that were filed against the Marcos family." Our research aims to analyze how the narrative that the Marcos family won all cases filed against them disseminated on Twitter.

Here's an overview of our project.

Research Question

  • Is there a correlation between the tweets claiming that the Marcos family won all cases filed against the Marcos family, and these tweets mentioning the Aquino family, the Liberal Party, or their supporters?
  • Is there a relationship between the frequency of these tweets and the electoral campaign of Ferdinand "Bongbong" Marcos Jr. for the 2022 Presidential Elections?

Hypothesis

  • Tweets claiming that the Marcoses won all cases are more likely to mention or not mention the Aquinos or the Liberal Party.
  • These tweets are also more frequent during the 2022 election campaign period.

Null Hpothesis

  • There is no correlation between the tweets claiming that the Marcos family won all cases filed against the Marcos family, and the tweets mentioning the Aquinos or the Liberal Party.
  • There is no correlation between the 2022 elections and the frequency of these tweets.

Action Plan: Analyze the users' tweets to see if they mention the Aquinos or Liberal Party and if there are any spike in tweets on a certain time period.

Let's talk about our data science methodology.

Topic

The Marcos family cleared all allegations that were filed against the Marcos family.

Keywords

We used combinations of these words, with one word per column (third and fourth columns were optional):
1: [Marcos,Imelda,Ferdinand]
2: [Won, Acquitted, Not Guilty, Dismiss, Junked, Lost, Panalo, Nanalo, Ibinasura]
3: [All, None, Not, Lahat, Wala]
4: [Case, Trial, Charges, Filed, Kaso]

Tools

We used snscrape to scrape tweets and other vital information from twitter. A script was used to get tweets using each of the 972 key phrases and save unique tweets to a CSV table.

Preprocessing

  • Unique accounts were collected to see metrics regarding the authors of the tweets, one such metric is the date the authors joined twitter relative to significant dates in this case, thy weree election campaign period.
  • Outliers were not removed; instead, the used to demonstrate that tweets receiving significant amounts of engagement were rare.
  • Tweets were manually translated to english.
  • Emoji and links in tweets were removed to facilitate automated analysis.

Statistical Test

The chi-square test is a statistical tool that examines the relationship between categorical variables. We used this in our research to test our hypotheses.

Machine Learning Model

Latent Dirichlet Allocation(LDA) is a topic modeling technique that can be used to analyze the context and identify underlying topics of the tweets. We used its results to gain valuable insights on tweets about our topic.

Date Distribution

- 89 tweets were posted by accounts within 12 months from joining Twitter.

- 38 accounts were made after Ferdinand Marcos Jr. filed for presidential candidacy.

Immoral Support

Tweets that were posted during the Presidential campaign period of Ferdinand "Bongbong" Marcos Jr. from his candidacy filing on October 2021 to the election on May 2022. This raises the possibility that a number of these tweets were politically motivated with the goal of raising support for Marcos during his campaign.

The Aquino Connection

Tweets that explicitly mention the Cory Aquino administration, the Aquino family, or members of the Liberal Party of the Philippines and their supporters. The Aquinos and the Liberal Party served as political opponents of Ferdinand Marcos Sr. and Bongbong Marcos respectively.

Historical Revisionism

Tweets mentioning the Aquinos or Liberal Party that portray them negatively using slurs or with accusations of corruption, plundering the national treasury, and conspiracy with the US government. These tweets support a narrative where the Marcos family was innocent of the accusations levied against them by a "villainous" Aquino administration.

The first test revealed a negative correlation for posts claiming the Marcoses won all of their cases and mentions of the Aquino family or the Liberal Party. One possible reason for this is that misinformation tweets aimed at people who are already biased in favor of the Marcos family or are neutral towards them would already have an idea of who the "opposition" is based on other misinformation or propaganda posts. As a result, mentioning a specific enemy such as the Aquino family would be unnecessary unless the goal is to provoke a response from the opposition.

Conclusion and Recommendations

  • There is enough evidence to suggest a link between Twitter posts claiming that the Marcos family won all cases that were filed against them and an explicit reference to the Aquino family, the Liberal Party or either of their supporters. However, the users were less likely to mention the Aquinos or Liberal Party, rather than more likely as initially predicted.
  • There is also evidence suggesting that the 2022 presidential campaign of Ferdinand Marcos Jr. influenced a spike in tweets spreading this misinformation on Twitter.
  • Further research can explore the relation between misinformation posts on social media related to the Marcos family or the Marcos administration, and the timing of these posts with high-profile events such as elections and the passing of controversial policies.

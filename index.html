Hi. We are Group-57.

And this is our project, Analyzing the proliferation of fake news: "The Marcos family cleared ALL allegations that were filed against the Marcos family." Our research aims to analyze how the narrative that the Marcos family won all cases filed against them disseminated on Twitter.

Problem Statement

One of the various forms of disinformation regarding the Marcos family that is being circulated is that the Marcoses won all of the cases that were filed against them, especially those partaining to stolen or ill-gotten wealth. This claim has been proven false by fact-checking organizations and court rulings confirming that several cases ended with the Marcos family being guilty of multiple instances of graft and corruption, and several million dollars worth of assets being repossessed by the government. Despite this, the claim circulated in social media sites such as Facebook and Twitter.

Our research aims to analyze how the narrative that the Marcos family won all cases filed against them disseminated on Twitter.

We believe that the results of this study will help in identifying patterns and trends common to misinformation centered around the Marcos family, especially in the context of historical revisionism with the aim of improving the image of the Marcoses and the administration of Ferdinand Marcos Sr. in the eyes of the Filipino public.

Research Question

  • Is there a correlation between the tweets claiming that the Marcos family won all cases filed against the Marcos family, and these tweets mentioning the Aquino family, the Liberal Party, or their supporters?
  • Is there a relationship between the frequency of these tweets and the electoral campaign of Ferdinand "Bongbong" Marcos Jr. for the 2022 Presidential Elections?

Hypothesis

  • Tweets claiming that the Marcoses won all cases are more likely to mention or not mention the Aquinos or the Liberal Party.
  • These tweets are also more frequent during the 2022 election campaign period.

Null Hpothesis

  • There is no correlation between the tweets claiming that the Marcos family won all cases filed against the Marcos family, and the tweets mentioning the Aquinos or the Liberal Party.
  • There is no correlation between the 2022 elections and the frequency of these tweets.

Action Plan: Analyze the users' tweets to see if they mention the Aquinos or Liberal Party and if there are any spike in tweets on a certain time period.

Data Collection

Let's talk about our data science methodology.

Topic

"The Marcos family cleared all allegations that were filed against the Marcos family."

Keywords

We used combinations of these words, with one word per column (third and fourth columns were optional):
1: [Marcos,Imelda,Ferdinand]
2: [Won, Acquitted, Not Guilty, Dismiss, Junked, Lost, Panalo, Nanalo, Ibinasura]
3: [All, None, Not, Lahat, Wala]
4: [Case, Trial, Charges, Filed, Kaso]

Tools

We used snscrape to scrape tweets and other vital information from twitter. A script was used to get tweets using each of the 972 key phrases and save unique tweets to a CSV table. Only tweets posted within the years 2016-2022 were gathered for this study.

Preprocessing

  • Unique accounts were collected to see metrics regarding the authors of the tweets, one such metric is the date the authors joined twitter relative to the 2022 election campaign period from the filing for presidential candidacy of Bongbong Marcos on October 2021 to the elections in May 2022.
  • Outliers were not removed; instead, the used to demonstrate that tweets receiving significant amounts of engagement were rare.
  • Tweets were manually translated to english.
  • Emoji and links in tweets were removed to facilitate automated analysis.

Statistical Test

The chi-square test is a statistical tool that examines the relationship between categorical variables. We used this in our research to test our hypotheses. Each data point was manually labeled on whether it mentioned the Aquinos, Liberal Party, or their supporters, while the date a tweet was posted was used to determine if the tweet was within the election campaign period.

Machine Learning Model

Latent Dirichlet Allocation(LDA) is a topic modeling technique that can be used to analyze the context and identify underlying topics of the tweets. We used its results to gain valuable insights on tweets about our topic. The dataset was grouped into 5 topic clusters with an α-value of 0.61 and a β-value of 0.91, as this combination was found to give the most consistent results.

Data Exploration

A total of 170 tweets were collected during data collection.

Date Distribution

38 of the accounts that posted this misinformation were made after Ferdinand Marcos Jr. filed for presidential candidacy. It is likely that some of these accounts were made specifically for posting pro-Marcos tweets during the election period.

Tweets Mentioning Aquinos

Out of 52 tweets mentioning the Aquinos, 27 of them were made within Ferdinand Marcos Jr.'s presidential campaign period. The months with the most tweets mentioning the Aquinos or Liberal Party also fall under this time period, with 6 tweets of this nature being posted on October 2021 and March 2022. The distribution suggests that these mentions become more prevalent during times of political conflict such as elections where supporters can rally against an opposition.

Age of Accounts from Tweet Date

This shows the number of accounts that posted a misinformation tweet about the Marcos family winning all cases within a certain number of months from joining Twitter.

89 tweets were posted by accounts within 12 months from joining Twitter. 31 tweets were posted by accounts within their first month.This skewed distribution suggests the possibility that some of these accounts are "troll accounts" that were made specifically to post these tweets and other kinds of misinformation.

Immoral Support

Tweets that were posted during the Presidential campaign period of Ferdinand "Bongbong" Marcos Jr. from his candidacy filing on October 2021 to the election on May 2022. This raises the possibility that a number of these tweets were politically motivated with the goal of raising support for Marcos during his campaign.

The Aquino Connection

Tweets that explicitly mention the Cory Aquino administration, the Aquino family, or members of the Liberal Party of the Philippines and their supporters. The Aquinos and the Liberal Party served as political opponents of Ferdinand Marcos Sr. and Bongbong Marcos respectively.

Historical Revisionism

Tweets mentioning the Aquinos or Liberal Party that portray them negatively using slurs or with accusations of corruption, plundering the national treasury, and conspiracy with the US government. These tweets support a narrative where the Marcos family was innocent of the accusations levied against them by a "villainous" Aquino administration.

Data Modeling

Here's what we found out from our tests.

Chi-Square Test

Crit. Val. = 3.841 ; α = 0.05 ; df = 1

First Test - Relation between tweets and mention of Aquinos/Liberal Party

For this test, the null hypothesis assumes that tweets are just as equally likely to mention the Aquinos or Liberal Party as they are to not mention either of them.

The expected values are (170/2)=85 for both categories.


Mentions Aquinos/Liberal Party? Observed Expected
Yes 52 85
No 118 85
Χ-stat: 25.62; p_val: 4.15e-7 → REJECT H0

The first test revealed a negative correlation for posts claiming the Marcoses won all of their cases and mentions of the Aquino family or the Liberal Party. This result is the opposite of our original prediction, but since the chi-squared value is greater than the critical value, and the p-value is very small, we can safely reject our null hypothesis that there is no relation between the tweets claiming that the Marcoses won all of their cases and the tweets mentioning the Aquinos, Liberal Party, or their supporters. One possible reason for this is that misinformation tweets aimed at people who are already biased in favor of the Marcos family or are neutral towards them would already have an idea of who the "opposition" is based on other misinformation or propaganda posts. As a result, mentioning a specific enemy such as the Aquino family would be unnecessary unless the goal is to provoke a response from the opposition or to convince non-supporters to agree to their theories.

Second Test - Relation between tweets and campaign period

For this test, the null hypothesis assumes that tweets claiming that the Marcoses won all their cases are equally likely to appear regardless of what month or year it is. The expected distributions were taken by multiplying the total number of tweets with the number of months in the interval divided by the total number of months in the observation period of 2016-2022, rounded to the nearest integer.

The expected values are thus (170*8/84) and (170*76/84), which round to 16 and 154 respectively

Posted During 2022 BBM Campaign? Observed Expected
Yes 103 16
No 67 154
Χ-stat: 522.21; p_val: 1.40e-115 → REJECT H0

The second test revealed a strong positive correlation between the posts claiming the Marcoses won all of their cases and the timing of Ferdinand Marcos Jr.'s presidential campaign in 2022. Since the chi-squared value is much greater than the critical value, and the p-value is very small, we can safely reject our null hypothesis that there is no relation between the tweets claiming that the Marcoses won all of their cases and the tweets taking place during the 2022 election campaign period of Bongbong Marcos. Rather, the data suggests that the Marcos campaign directly led to a surge in these tweets, mainly by Bongbong Marcos supporters as indicated by the activity of many of the users that posted these tweets, as well as troll accounts as indicated by the high proportion of users that posted these tweets within a year of joining.

Latent Dirichlet Allocation

The topics were clustered into 5 groups. The applet shows the most relevant terms found in each group. Larger circles indicate more tweets in the group, while the red and blue bars correspond to the number of mentions of a keyword in the group and in all groups, respectively.

Topic Classification

The five groups (with an example tweet) are as follows:.

1. The Marcoses were cleared in court of their cases with Imelda Marcos'es victory in the Trial of the Century in New York.

2. The Sandiganbayan and Philippine Courts were not able to prove after so many years that the Marcos wealth was ill-gotten or that the Marcoses were theives.

3. The Aquinos still have no proof that the Marcoses stole from the people after so many years, if anything the Aquinos were the real theives who stole the Marcos family's hard-earned wealth.

4. There is no evidence that the Marcoses are guilty of anything, as shown in the New York trial, and all the accusations against them are baseless conjecture.

5. The courts and the Philippine Commision on Good Governance had no evidence against the Marcoses, so they deserve to have their stolen wealth returned to them.

In all of these groups, the common argument is that because the Marcos family, more specifically Imelda Marcos, won so many high-profile cases, they are automatically cleared of all charges. This is a flawed argument, since these victories do not invalidate the multiple lost cases that the Marcoses incurred over the years. From a political perspective, the goal of some of these posts is not just to prove the innocence of the Marcos family, but to also portray their opposition as corrupt and villainous.

It should be emphaized that these tweets are part of a movement to distort Philippine history through false claims and portray the administration of Ferdinand Marcos Sr. as a "golden age" without the corruption, human rights violations, and criminal charges that have happened and have been repeatedly verified to have happened in reality. While misinformation is not strictly tied to the Marcos administrations and may even be used by opposing parties, it remains a prominent and frequently-used tool to gain favor from the masses. Identifying and immediately addressing these blatant falsehoods, especially when they are promoted by those in positions of power and influence, will be essential to upholding truth and democracy in Philippine society.

Conclusion and Recommendations

  • There is enough evidence to suggest a link between Twitter posts claiming that the Marcos family won all cases that were filed against them and an explicit reference to the Aquino family, the Liberal Party or either of their supporters. However, the users were less likely to mention the Aquinos or Liberal Party, rather than more likely as initially predicted.
  • There is also evidence suggesting that the 2022 presidential campaign of Ferdinand Marcos Jr. influenced a spike in tweets spreading this misinformation on Twitter.
  • Further research can explore the relation between misinformation posts on social media related to the Marcos family or the Marcos administration, and the timing of these posts with high-profile events such as elections and the passing of controversial policies.
  • We should be critical about posts and claims made on social media and conduct research and validation before even considering sharing these claims with others, even when these claims support our biases and political agendas.

About Us

Our group chose the topic of the misinformation that the Marcos family won all of the cases filed against them for our project as this claim represents the dangers of unchecked misinformation on the internet and social media, as well as the ulterior goals of pro-Marcos disinformation movements to clear them of the atrocities and rampant corruption of the Martial Law era. It also highlights the binary absolutism and radicalization that these forms of misinformation promote, in this case the premise that the Marcoses and their allied politicians are righteous and completely innocent while the "Dilawan" liberal politicians and their supporters are corrupt or foolish for opposing the Marcoses.
